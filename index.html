SpiderLine (aka SpiderVine)

SpiderLine is a human-powered, line-based access and movement system designed for regenerative work in environments where care, repairability, and restraint matter more than speed or scale.

What This Is

SpiderLine supports forestry, camps and crossings, shoreline and reef tending, and other contexts where ground disturbance or heavy machinery are undesirable.

1-Cable SpiderLine

  • Dynamic, skill-based system
  • Stability achieved through posture, momentum management, and braking
  • Minimal materials, higher learning curve

2-Cable SpiderLine

  • Geometry-based stability
  • Load shared across two parallel cables
  • Higher carrying capacity and repeatability

Both forms are human-powered first and designed to fail visibly rather than catastrophically.

Companion Systems

The Spoovel — a field interface tool for probing, tending, measuring, placing, and retrieving.

Garden Raft — a floating staging and continuity platform for shoreline, reef, and wetland contexts.

Spoovel Winch Module (optional) — a manual-first attachment enabling controlled hauling, pulley systems, emergency stabilization, and marine waste retrieval.

Together they form a simple regenerative circuit:

access → action → return → adjust

Design Ethic

  • Human-powered first
  • Field-repairable with basic tools
  • No single point of failure
  • Clear training assumptions
  • Capacity claims are always conditional
  • Safety tether and capture geometry are non-negotiable

SpiderLine does not pretend gravity is optional.
